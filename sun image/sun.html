About the Sun

The Sun is a massive ball of burning gases and plasma located at the center of our solar system. It provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth and plays a crucial role in the orbits of planets, asteroids, and comets.

It is mostly made up of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%). The Sun's surface temperature is around 5,500°C (9,932°F), while the core can reach over 15 million°C!

The energy from the Sun is created by a process called nuclear fusion, where hydrogen atoms fuse to form helium and release energy. This energy takes thousands of years to reach the surface and only about 8 minutes to travel from the Sun to Earth.

Without the Sun, life would not exist on Earth. It drives our climate, weather, ocean currents, and helps plants grow through photosynthesis.
